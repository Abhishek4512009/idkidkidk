Gundu Raghav

The Thunder of Tamil Nadu – A Journey Through Cinema, Comedy & Change

Early Life

Born Raghavendran Subramaniam on March 3, 1972, in Madurai, Gundu Raghav came from a humble weaver's family. He gained popularity for his humor and mimicry in village festivals, earning the nickname “Gundu”.

Rise to Stardom

He debuted in the 1990s, and his performance in Thanni Thotti Theepori made him a household name. With witty dialogue and slapstick humor, Gundu Raghav became a fan favorite across Tamil Nadu.

Political Career

In 2010, he founded Tamizh Thimiru Kazhagam and became an MLA. Known for creative reforms like cinema tickets for seniors and stand-up contests, his slogan "Semma Weightu!" is still remembered.

Fun Facts

  • Owns a parrot named Rajini that mimics film dialogues
  • Released a folk hit titled "Sambar in My Soul"
  • Holds a doctorate in “Performative Humor”
  • Trained in Kalari martial arts

Legacy

Gundu Raghav remains a beloved cultural figure in Tamil Nadu. His birthday is celebrated with film marathons and theater tributes.

“I never wanted to be a star. I just wanted to make Madurai laugh. Stardom was the byproduct of sincerity.” – Gundu Raghav
